üè∏ Badminton Indoor/Outdoor

Badminton is a fast-paced racquet sport that combines agility, strategy, and precision. Perfect for improving cardiovascular health, hand-eye coordination, and reflexes while having fun. Can be played casually with friends or competitively.

Game Rules

  • Scoring: A match consists of the best of three games. The first player/team to reach 21 points wins a game. Points are scored when the shuttlecock lands in the opponent's court or when they commit a fault.
  • Serving: The server must hit the shuttlecock below waist height and keep both feet on the ground. The serve must go diagonally across the court to the opponent's service box.
  • Court Boundaries: The shuttlecock must land within the court boundaries. Different boundary lines apply for singles and doubles play. A shuttlecock landing on the line is considered in.
  • Lets and Faults: A 'let' is called when play is interrupted (like a shuttlecock from another court). Faults include hitting the net, touching the net with your racket or body, and hitting the shuttlecock before it crosses to your side.
  • Change of Ends: Players change ends after each game. In the third game, players switch when the leading score reaches 11 points.
  • Equipment: You'll need a lightweight racket, shuttlecocks (feather or synthetic), proper court shoes with good grip, and comfortable athletic clothing that allows free movement.
